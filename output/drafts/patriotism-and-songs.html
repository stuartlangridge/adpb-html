Patriotism and songs

I’ve been thinking about patriotism, and looking at songs about one’s country that were written a hundred years ago.

Let’s, for now, ignore what people think patriotism means today. Instead, let’s look at those words that moved us and spoke to what our country was, whichever country that is, a century ago.

Penn and Teller do a magic trick where they burn an American flag. They’ve reiterated the patter for this a thousand times. But they also did it on the West Wing, and there what they said was presumably altered by the scriptwriters. In particular, Penn recites wonderfully and impactfully some lines from The Star-Spangled Banner, some of Francis Scott Key’s most famous words:

Now it catches the gleam of the morning’s first beam
In full glory reflected now shines on the stream
‘Tis the star-spangled banner
oh, long may it wave
O’er the land of the free
and the home of the brave.
