The King's Experience

(THIS IS A DRAFT; PLEASE DON'T SHARE IT AROUND)

There is a branch of user experience design that we might call KX, for the King’s Experience. This is where the user experience you provide to your users is that they can just sit on a big throne and command a thing done and lo, it is done. Like Joffrey Baratheon. Wave a hand and it happens as if by magic.

This is fine and great and marvellous if the magic is actually a Python script or something. But sometimes, it’s not a script. It’s a person. And that’s not actually saving any work. It certainly provides a brilliant user experience to the king, who gets to believe that he speaks a desire and the universe spontaneously responds, but what actually happens is that some peon does the work that the king commands. The work still has to be done: all you’ve achieved is to shift it to someone else. That person might be cheaper, or more willing, but don’t kid yourself that outsourcing a problem makes it go away. I dislike “UX” which gives the impression that it runs on magic when it actually runs by giving your task to some less fortunate person and not telling you that that happened.

Now, it’s possible to take this too far; I mean, if taken to extremes this is an argument against the whole concept of capitalism. And who wants one of those, eh? (A forest of hands go up, mine maybe included...) But if you’re giving users the impression that things happen with zero effort but they actually happen with a bunch of effort provided by those you’ve designated as cheaper less-important people… maybe take a moment to think about the wider ramifications of this world you're creating. This world you’re implying your users should want to aspire to. The king is not a normal user. KX might be a nice UX, but it's not a good one.
